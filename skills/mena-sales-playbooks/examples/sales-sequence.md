## Context

- Market: Saudi Arabia.
- Buyer: enterprise operations director at a private logistics company.
- Channel: email first; WhatsApp only if the prospect opted in or there is prior context.
- Stage: first-touch planning.
- Risk tier: enterprise, procurement-aware, not government unless discovered.

## Source-backed constraints

- Country and procurement context matter; do not treat Saudi Arabia as a generic GCC sales context.
- If the buyer is government or semi-government, check procurement route, supplier registration, tender needs, security/legal review, and local partner requirements before outreach is finalized.
- WhatsApp requires permission, platform-policy review, and opt-out handling before use.

## Assumptions

- The contact is a business contact gathered from a lawful source.
- No customer data, money movement, bank data, health data, payroll data, or government data is being requested.
- No local customer reference is approved yet.

## Email 1 draft

```text
Subject: Reducing manual operations follow-up across branches

Hi [Name],

I am [Sender] from [Company]. We help operations teams reduce manual follow-up around [specific workflow] without replacing their existing systems.

For logistics teams, the first useful step is usually a short workflow map: where requests enter, where they wait, who owns exceptions, and what reports leadership needs.

Would it be worth a 20-minute call next week to compare that map with your current process?

If you are not the right owner, I would appreciate being pointed to the correct operations or process lead.

Regards,
[Sender]
```

## Follow-up 1 draft

```text
Subject: Re: Reducing manual operations follow-up across branches

Hi [Name],

One practical artifact we can share before a call is a one-page checklist for spotting where manual operations follow-up becomes expensive: duplicate entry, unclear ownership, missed SLA alerts, and reporting gaps.

Should I send that checklist, or is there another owner for this process?

Regards,
[Sender]
```

## WhatsApp follow-up draft

Use only if the buyer gave permission or there is reviewed prior context.

```text
Hi [Name], this is [Sender] from [Company]. We connected about operations follow-up for [specific context]. Would it be useful if I sent the one-page workflow checklist by email?

If WhatsApp is not the right channel, I can stop here.
```

## Operator review flags

- Confirm contact source and consent before use.
- Confirm whether the account is private enterprise, government contractor, or government/semi-government.
- Do not add Saudi Arabic wording, local etiquette claims, or seniority-specific phrasing without native/operator review.
