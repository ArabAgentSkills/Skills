# WhatsApp Follow-Up

Use WhatsApp only as a permission-aware follow-up channel. Do not use it for scraped cold lists or mass outreach.

## Required Checks

- Prior relationship, inbound request, event conversation, opted-in contact, or reviewed lawful basis.
- Correct sender identity and company identity.
- Message category, template needs, account quality, messaging limits, and platform policy are reviewed.
- Opt-out handling is available.
- The operator can stop messaging immediately if the contact objects.

## Safe Pattern

1. Identify yourself and the company.
2. Reference the specific prior context.
3. Keep it short.
4. Ask one question or offer one next step.
5. Avoid attachments unless approved.
6. Avoid pressure, impersonation, deceptive urgency, and unsupported claims.

## Draft Template

```text
Hi [Name], this is [Sender] from [Company].

We spoke at [specific context] about [specific business issue]. Would it be useful if I sent a short [demo/checklist/one-page note] for [outcome]?

If WhatsApp is not the right channel, I can follow up by email or stop here.
```

## Do Not Use WhatsApp For

- Scraped contact lists.
- Bulk campaigns without opt-in and platform review.
- Government or regulated-buyer pressure.
- Sensitive contract negotiation.
- Pricing, legal, compliance, procurement, banking, health, tax, or identity claims that have not been reviewed.

## Review Flags

Country-specific privacy, telecom, DND, sender-ID, template, and account-quality rules can change. Mark as `Needs platform/account access` and `Needs local legal review` before live use.
