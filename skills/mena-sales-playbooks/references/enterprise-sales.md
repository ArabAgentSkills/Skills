# Enterprise Sales

Use for banks, insurers, telcos, large retailers, logistics companies, universities, hospitals, family offices, holding companies, large government contractors, and complex B2B accounts.

## Account Map

Track these roles when known:

- Economic buyer.
- Technical buyer.
- Operations owner.
- Internal champion.
- Procurement.
- Legal.
- Information security.
- Finance.
- Data/privacy owner.
- Local partner or sponsor if required.

## Sales Workflow

1. Confirm whether the buyer can purchase directly or needs vendor registration, tender, approved supplier status, or partner participation.
2. Draft first contact around the business problem and credible proof, not product features alone.
3. Prepare discovery before proposing.
4. After meetings, send factual recaps with owners, dates, open questions, and procurement/security/legal next steps.
5. Do not claim compliance, integration readiness, data residency, or customer references without source-backed evidence.

## Discovery Agenda

1. Confirm role and context.
2. Confirm the problem in the buyer's words.
3. Ask how the workflow works today.
4. Ask what happens if the problem stays unresolved.
5. Ask who else is involved in evaluation, procurement, security, legal, finance, and operations.
6. Ask what compliance, data, hosting, security, and budget requirements apply.
7. Summarize back and agree the next step.

## Approval Gates

Government, semi-government, banking, open banking, payments, telecom, identity, tax, payroll, health, education, logistics, or critical infrastructure buyers require operator review before final outreach, proposal language, document sharing, or follow-up.

## Avoid

- Pushing for commitment before procurement path is known.
- Offering discounts, commissions, gifts, introductions, or shortcuts.
- Sharing production credentials, customer lists, security artifacts, logs, or live data during sales.
