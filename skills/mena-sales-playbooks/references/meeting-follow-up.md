# Meeting Follow-Up

Meeting follow-up should be factual, low-pressure, and clearly marked as a draft until approved.

## Standard Structure

1. Thank attendees.
2. Summarize the business problem and desired outcome.
3. List decisions made.
4. List open questions.
5. Name owners and due dates.
6. Confirm the next step and date.
7. Attach only approved materials.
8. Mark assumptions explicitly.

## Enterprise Additions

For enterprise, government, semi-government, or regulated buyers, add:

- Procurement route and vendor-registration status.
- Security and legal review owner.
- Data, hosting, integration, and access questions.
- Budget owner and budget-cycle uncertainty.
- Any tender portal, local entity, Arabic-documentation, or partner dependency.

## Draft Template

```text
Subject: Recap and next step from [meeting/topic]

Hi [Names],

Thank you for the discussion today. My understanding is:

- Current problem: [buyer words]
- Desired outcome: [outcome]
- Decisions made: [decisions or "none yet"]
- Open questions: [questions]
- Owners: [owner/date]
- Next step: [single next step/date]

Assumptions to confirm: [assumptions]

Regards,
[Sender]
```

## Avoid

- Saying the buyer approved something they did not approve.
- Creating urgency that was not agreed.
- Naming customers, partners, government entities, banks, or telcos without permission.
- Attaching sensitive files, security docs, price sheets, or contracts without approval.
