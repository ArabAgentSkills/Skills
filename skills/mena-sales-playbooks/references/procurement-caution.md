# Procurement Caution

Use this when the buyer is enterprise, government, semi-government, public sector, regulated, or procurement-led. This file is not legal advice.

## Universal Cautions

- A strong champion is not enough if procurement, legal, security, finance, or data/privacy cannot approve.
- Tender portals, supplier registration, local entity requirements, Arabic documentation, partner participation, prequalification, and security review can be deal-specific.
- Public-sector and semi-government conversations can create anti-bribery, gift, conflict-of-interest, sanctions, and facilitation-payment exposure.
- Any request for commission, success fee, gift, shortcut, private access, or guaranteed government introduction requires operator and legal review.

## Source-Backed Country Notes

These are caution notes, not universal local sales norms.

### Saudi Arabia

- Check Etimad and official Ministry of Finance routes for government procurement.
- ITA guidance supports caution around local presence, after-sales service, and agent/distributor expectations in some sectors.
- Finance/open-banking-related work should check SAMA and relevant licensing or sandbox/production routes.
- Do not assume one Saudi or GCC sales script fits all sectors or buyers.

### UAE

- Federal procurement work should check Ministry of Finance supplier registration and the Digital Procurement Platform.
- Emirate-level, free-zone, private-enterprise, and family-business contexts can differ.
- Distributor and agent terms can carry legal and termination complexity; use local legal review.

### Egypt

- ITA guidance supports caution around public-sector complexity and local agent/distributor roles in some channels.
- SME/agency selling can stay practical and proof-led, but do not infer WhatsApp or dialect norms without review.

### Qatar

- Check the official government procurement portal and tender-specific requirements.
- Public-sector, energy, government, and semi-government work needs procurement and local legal review before outreach language is finalized.

### Other Markets

- Mark detailed procurement routes as `Unknown from public docs` until official portals, country guides, or local expert review are checked.
- Use `Needs procurement review` for public-sector or semi-government work even when a public portal exists.

## Output Rule

When this file applies, include:

- Procurement route known/unknown.
- Supplier registration known/unknown.
- Tender or portal dependency.
- Local partner/legal review need.
- Security, privacy, finance, and data owners.
- Blockers before any submission or formal proposal.
