# B2B Outbound

Use for first-touch email, LinkedIn-style draft copy, call prep, and light follow-up planning for business contacts. Draft only; do not send.

## Intake

- Country or market.
- Buyer segment and sector.
- Persona and likely business problem.
- Contact source and permission status.
- Prior relationship or event context.
- Channel: email, WhatsApp, phone, meeting, or proposal.
- Sales stage and desired next step.

## Safe Outbound Pattern

1. State sender identity clearly.
2. Reference a specific business context that is true.
3. Offer one relevant outcome or hypothesis.
4. Use one low-pressure call to action.
5. Include opt-out or permission-aware wording where outbound applies.
6. Mark country tone, dialect, etiquette, and seniority assumptions for native/operator review.

## Sequence Shape

- Touch 1: short relevance note and one CTA.
- Touch 2: useful proof asset, checklist, or concrete question.
- Touch 3: permission-aware close-the-loop note.

Stop if the recipient objects, opts out, or the contact source is unclear.

## Do Not Include

- Fake familiarity or implied relationship.
- Unsupported ROI, compliance, procurement, or government-access claims.
- Invented local references, case studies, logos, integrations, or partnerships.
- Pressure based on status, fear, religion, nationality, family, hardship, or urgency.

## Review Flags

- Scraped contact list, unclear consent, consumer contacts, minors, patients, students, employees, or sensitive personal data.
- Government, semi-government, financial services, telecom, health, education, identity, tax, payroll, or public procurement buyers.
- Requests to send, automate, sequence, upload, enrich, or retarget contacts.
