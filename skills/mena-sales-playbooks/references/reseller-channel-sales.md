# Reseller and Channel Sales

Use for referral, reseller, channel partner, agency bundle, implementation partner, and local distributor motions.

## Motion Types

- Referral: partner introduces qualified opportunities; vendor owns sales and delivery.
- Reseller: partner sells under agreed commercial terms.
- Implementation partner: partner implements, configures, localizes, or trains.
- Agency bundle: product is packaged inside an agency service.
- Distributor/channel partner: partner handles market development, sales, and sometimes support.

## Default Strategy

1. Start with referral or pilot-first language.
2. Avoid exclusivity until performance, territory, support capacity, and legal terms are reviewed.
3. Define who owns sales, onboarding, implementation, support, billing, compliance, and customer success.
4. Separate commercial fit from regulated feasibility.
5. Keep customer data, logo use, references, and non-circumvention boundaries explicit.

## Due Diligence Questions

- Which customer segment does the partner already serve?
- What proof exists beyond a verbal network claim?
- Who will generate leads and qualify opportunities?
- Who owns proposal, implementation, support, and renewals?
- What customer data will the partner access?
- Is the partner asking for exclusivity, territory, reseller rights, sublicensing, revenue share, or public logo use?

## High-Risk Partner Terms

- Exclusivity or territory lock-in.
- Commission or success fee tied to government or regulated buyers.
- Revenue share, sublicensing, sub-resellers, customer ownership, or non-circumvention.
- Customer data access, production API access, support impersonation, or shared credentials.
- Claims of guaranteed access to banks, telcos, ministries, regulators, or public-sector buyers.

## Review Flags

Use local legal and operator review for commercial agency law, termination rights, tax treatment, anti-bribery, regulated data, public procurement, and country-specific partner obligations.
