# Complaint De-escalation

Use this file for angry customers, public complaints, repeated unresolved cases, and sensitive escalation.

## De-escalation Pattern

1. Acknowledge the issue without arguing.
2. Use empathy without over-admitting liability when facts are unknown.
3. Move private details to a secure channel.
4. Create or reference a case ID.
5. State the next review step and follow-up placeholder.
6. Escalate when required.

## General Complaint Macro

Required inputs:

- `[case_id]`
- `[next_followup_at]`
- `[secure_link]` when sensitive data is needed

Arabic:

> نتفهم انزعاجك ونشكرك على توضيح المشكلة. سجلنا شكواك برقم `[case_id]` وسنراجع التفاصيل مع الفريق المختص. سنعود لك بتحديث في `[next_followup_at]`. إذا كانت الشكوى تتعلق بدفع أو بيانات حساب، سننقل المتابعة إلى قناة آمنة: `[secure_link]`.

English mirror:

> We understand your frustration and thank you for explaining the issue. We registered complaint `[case_id]` and will review it with the right team. We will update you at `[next_followup_at]`. If it involves payment or account data, we will move the follow-up to `[secure_link]`.

## Public Comment Macro

Arabic:

> نتفهم انزعاجك ونريد مساعدتك. حفاظًا على خصوصيتك، أرسلنا لك طريقة تواصل خاصة لمراجعة التفاصيل بأمان.

English mirror:

> We understand your frustration and want to help. To protect your privacy, we sent you a private way to share details securely.

Do not include:

- Order number.
- Phone, address, or email.
- Payment or card details.
- Identity or tax documents.
- Blame, sarcasm, or argument.

## Escalate Immediately

- Legal threat or regulator complaint.
- Discrimination, harassment, safety, medical, or public-safety concern.
- Fraud allegation.
- Payment, identity, tax, or account-access issue.
- Public complaint gaining visibility.
- Customer rejects first resolution or the case repeats.
