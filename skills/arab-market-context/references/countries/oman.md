## Audience and segment notes

- Scope every recommendation by segment, channel, and buyer/user role.
- Treat public indicators as context signals, not as proof of individual behavior or channel preference.
- Mark operator hypotheses clearly when public sources do not answer the question.

## Common channels

- Discovery channels to consider: WhatsApp, phone, email.
- Exact channel priority by audience and product category needs local/operator review.

## Digital and mobile context

- Use World Bank, ITU, GSMA, DataReportal, and national statistics/open-data sources for digital context.
- Do not infer checkout, support, or channel behavior from aggregate connectivity statistics alone.

## Payment and checkout expectations

- Keep card, wallet, bank transfer, cash-on-delivery, payment link, and assisted checkout as discovery options until product, vendor, and segment evidence is checked.
- Route implementation details to the relevant infrastructure skill such as mena-payments, mena-bnpl, or arab-einvoicing-tax.

## Logistics and delivery context

- Address, phone, city, district, landmark, pickup/return, and delivery-note requirements should be validated against carrier/vendor docs.
- Do not force non-local address assumptions without checking product and logistics context.

## Support channel context

- Start with the provided channels as discovery options, then confirm by segment and business model.
- Do not promise refunds, delivery timelines, account actions, or regulated outcomes unless the user provides policy and approval.

## Trust signals

- Consider local language support, clear pricing/currency, visible policy terms, invoices/tax clarity, known payment/logistics options, and local contact paths when relevant.
- Do not invent compliance, partnership, endorsement, or certification claims.

## Marketing timing notes

- Use Ramadan, Eid, national days, school seasons, shopping periods, and local holidays only with date status and source confidence.
- Religious or national-symbol campaigns need sensitivity review and official calendar confirmation where timing matters.

## Public holidays and seasonality

- Use Nager.Date, date-holidays, or Hijri libraries as planning leads only.
- Future Ramadan and Eid dates are estimated unless an official country source confirms them.

## B2B sales notes

- Ask for buyer type, sector, sales stage, permission/consent state, procurement exposure, and government/semi-government involvement before drafting.
- Country-level context should shape questions and risk flags, not create universal sales norms.

## Product localization notes

- Check RTL/bidi behavior, Arabic copy, locale formatting, phone/address forms, currency, VAT wording, calendar assumptions, privacy/ID fields, and accessibility.
- Use CLDR, Unicode bidi, W3C ALREQ, libphonenumber, libaddressinput, Intl/FormatJS, and official country sources where applicable.

## Risks and caveats

- Avoid stereotypes, unsupported certainty, religious overclaiming, legal/compliance claims without official sources, and political persuasion targeting.
- Public brand copy in dialect, high-stakes support macros, and strategic market recommendations need native or expert review.
- Do not store reviewer PII or private survey responses in public files.
