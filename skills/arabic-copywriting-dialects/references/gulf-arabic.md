# Gulf And Saudi Arabic

## Use Cases

Use `Saudi Arabic` for Saudi-specific drafts. Use `Gulf Arabic` only as a rough planning label before country-specific review.

Do not collapse Saudi, Emirati, Kuwaiti, Qatari, Bahraini, and Omani usage into one production voice.

## Required Caution

- Treat Gulf and Saudi copy as `draft/native_review_required`.
- Require country-specific review for public, paid, support, sales, and brand-critical copy.
- Use MSA for formal, regulated, government, financial, healthcare, tax, identity, HR, app UI, and B2B content unless local strategy says otherwise.
- Review religious, national, family, gender, and formality signals carefully.

## Drafting Rules

- Prefer clear MSA or lightly localized wording before colloquial-heavy copy.
- Label Saudi copy as Saudi-specific when Saudi Arabia is the market.
- For UAE, Kuwait, Qatar, Bahrain, and Oman, do not infer idioms or tone from public datasets alone.
- Keep bilingual glossary decisions explicit for SaaS, fintech, logistics, and operations content.

## Native Review Questions

- Is the country label correct, or should the copy be split by Saudi/UAE/Kuwait/Qatar/Bahrain/Oman?
- Does the formality level match the buyer role and brand?
- Are religious, national, or family references avoided unless approved?
- Is mixed Arabic-English terminology expected in this sector?
