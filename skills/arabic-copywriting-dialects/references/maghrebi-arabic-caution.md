# Maghrebi Arabic Caution

## Default Position

Maghrebi varieties can differ substantially from MSA and from Mashriqi dialects. French, Tamazight/Amazigh influence, Arabizi, and code-switching patterns vary by country, audience, and channel.

Default to MSA or locally reviewed Moroccan, Algerian, Tunisian, or Libyan copy.

## Do Not Do

- Do not generate publish-ready Maghrebi brand copy from public sources alone.
- Do not treat Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia, Libya, and Mauritania as one voice.
- Do not assume French or Arabizi is acceptable.
- Do not use a slogan, joke, idiom, or social post without native review.

## Safer Outputs

- MSA draft for cross-market use.
- Native-review brief for the target country.
- Glossary questions for Arabic, French, English, Tamazight/Amazigh, and product terms.
- Copy QA checklist instead of final dialect wording.

## If A Draft Is Required

Label it:

- `language_variety`: market-specific Maghrebi draft, if the user specifies country.
- `status`: `draft/native_review_required`.
- `risk_tier`: `high_risk_brand_copy` for slogans, ads, landing-page hero copy, humor, and social posts.
- `do_not_publish_until`: local native reviewer approves wording for country, audience, channel, and brand.

## Native Review Questions

- Which country and city/audience is targeted?
- Should the final copy use MSA, local Arabic, French, bilingual, or another approved format?
- Are there accessibility, search, moderation, or brand risks from Arabizi/code-switching?
- Are any terms politically, socially, or culturally loaded?
