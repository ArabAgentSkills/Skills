# Native Review Rules

## Native Review Is Required For

- Any dialect copy.
- Slogans, hero copy, campaign lines, ads, social posts, influencer scripts, and public apologies.
- Humor, idioms, slang, religious references, national symbols, cultural references, or sensitive timing.
- Sales follow-up, WhatsApp, SMS, email, support macros, and escalation replies.
- Bilingual Arabic-English copy where glossary, code-switching, or status tone matters.
- Maghrebi and Arabizi copy.

## Expert Review Is Required For

- Legal, tax, medical, healthcare, credit, investment, financing, payroll, employment, housing, government, identity, privacy, political, religious, public safety, or crisis content.
- Platform policy and paid-ad launch decisions.
- Survey/reviewer evidence publication.

## Review Scope

Do not mark copy as globally reviewed. Approval must name:

- Country or market.
- Language variety.
- Audience.
- Channel.
- Use case.
- Reviewer role.
- Review date.
- Known limitations.

## Reviewer Brief

Ask the reviewer:

- Does this sound natural and respectful for the target audience?
- Is it too formal, too casual, class-coded, sarcastic, gendered, or pushy?
- Are any words politically, religiously, culturally, or socially loaded?
- Should any terms stay in English or use a local synonym?
- Would you approve this for the exact channel and campaign?

## Output Labels

- `draft/native_review_required`: use for all unreviewed dialect examples.
- `reviewed_native_scoped`: use only with reviewer scope and date.
- `expert_review_required`: use for regulated or sensitive claims.
- `do_not_use_without_local_review`: use when market nuance is unknown or risk is high.
