# Landing Page Copy

## Default Language

Use MSA for cross-market B2B, SaaS, education, government, finance, healthcare, logistics, HR, tax, identity, and trust-building pages. Use locally reviewed dialect for country-specific consumer landing pages when warmth or relatability is a strategic goal.

## Section Rules

- Hero: direct benefit, not idiom-heavy unless locally reviewed.
- Subheading: audience and outcome in concrete terms.
- Proof: claims must be source-backed and scoped.
- Pricing/CTA: clear action verbs and no ambiguous commitments.
- FAQ: MSA or simple local Arabic; no legal claims unless reviewed.

## High-Risk Elements

- Slogans.
- Hero headlines.
- Testimonials.
- Performance, revenue, health, finance, tax, legal, or compliance claims.
- Pricing, discounts, refunds, trial terms, and guarantees.
- Paid landing pages connected to ads.

## Draft Pattern

```text
language_variety: MSA
status: safe_for_draft
review_required: brand + legal/expert if claims are regulated

Headline: [clear outcome]
Subheading: [who it is for + concrete result]
CTA: [single action]
Proof note: [source-backed proof only, or remove]
```

For dialect variants, change status to `draft/native_review_required/high_risk_brand_copy`.

## QA Checklist

- Claims have evidence or are removed.
- Dialect variants are not reused across countries without review.
- CTA does not imply hidden commitment.
- RTL layout is screenshot-tested on desktop and mobile.
- Paid pages match ad language and platform policy expectations.
