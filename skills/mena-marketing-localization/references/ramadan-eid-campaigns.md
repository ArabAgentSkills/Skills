# Ramadan And Eid Campaigns

Use this reference for Ramadan, Eid al-Fitr, Eid al-Adha, Hajj-adjacent, and national-day campaign planning.

## Date Basis

| Date basis | Use |
| --- | --- |
| Fixed Gregorian | Can be planned as recurring, but observed closures still need annual source checks. |
| Official annual announcement | Use only for the year, country, and sector named by the source. |
| Hijri estimated | Planning lead only. Mark as estimated until local official confirmation. |
| Hijri official confirmed | Use after country-specific official confirmation and source URL are recorded. |
| Sector calendar | Banks, schools, exchanges, and government calendars apply only to that sector. |
| Retailer season | Commercial hypothesis, not a public holiday or country-wide behavior claim. |

## Scoped Notes

- Country: Egypt. Segment: B2C/app/ecommerce. Channel: Ramadan calendar, mobile web, social, search. Confidence: A for Egypt-specific usage-change evidence. Evidence: NTRA Ramadan 2024 usage index. Use as a planning signal only; do not extrapolate to all countries.
- Country: Saudi Arabia. Segment: finance/ecommerce/logistics/support. Channel: Ramadan/Eid operating calendar. Confidence: A for SAMA bank/payment-sector schedules and MOFA National Day date. Evidence: SAMA and MOFA official pages. Use sector scope; do not generalize bank hours to all businesses.
- Country: Morocco. Segment: all public campaigns. Channel: Ramadan/Eid/national-day calendar. Confidence: A for listed public holidays, needs native review for creative use. Evidence: Moroccan ministry public holiday page. Use local review for language, symbols, and timing.
- Country: UAE. Segment: public-sector/support/ecommerce. Channel: Eid/public holiday planning. Confidence: A for official federal-government circulars where cited, needs review for private-sector scope. Evidence: u.ae, FAHR, MoHRE source leads.

## Campaign Periods

Only use these as planning buckets, not performance claims:

- Pre-Ramadan preparation.
- Ramadan start.
- Ramadan middle period.
- Last ten days.
- Eid preparation.
- Eid holiday period.
- Post-Eid return to normal operations.

## Required Review Gates

- Native and Muslim/cultural review for greetings, fasting, prayer, charity, family, sacrifice, Hajj, and religious imagery.
- Legal/compliance review for finance, health, charity, food, travel, children, claims, discounts, and regulated products.
- Operations review for support hours, delivery, payment settlement, returns, inventory, and customer-service scripts.
- Platform review for ads, landing pages, creator content, retargeting, and outbound messages.

## Avoid

- Treating Hajj as a generic sales moment.
- Making religious rulings or implying religious authority.
- Jokes about fasting, prayer, charity, sacrifice, hardship, or family obligation.
- Using flags, rulers, armed forces, political slogans, founding narratives, or conflict references without local review.
