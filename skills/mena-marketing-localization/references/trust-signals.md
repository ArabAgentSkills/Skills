# Trust Signals

Use this reference for localizing visible trust, proof, and assurance elements.

## Trust Signal Inventory

| Signal | Required scope |
| --- | --- |
| Business identity | Country, legal name, local address/contact, tax/VAT where applicable. |
| Payments | Country, segment, checkout channel, source-backed payment methods or unknown. |
| Delivery | Country, city/region, delivery promise, carrier or fulfillment scope. |
| Returns/refunds | Country, ecommerce/category, support and policy owner. |
| Support | Language, channel, hours, holiday coverage, escalation. |
| Privacy | Country, consent, retargeting, direct marketing, opt-out. |
| Reviews/testimonials | Source, consent, disclosure, no PII, no fake social proof. |
| Certifications | Official source only; no invented badges. |

## Scoped Notes

- Country: UAE. Segment: ecommerce/marketplace. Channel: landing page/checkout. Confidence: B. Evidence: EZDubai/Euromonitor release notes payment, logistics, and returns as ecommerce factors. Agent implication: make payment, delivery, and returns visible and locally accurate.
- Country: Egypt. Segment: ecommerce/app. Channel: WhatsApp/CRM/landing page. Confidence: A for direct-marketing consent risk existence. Evidence: Egypt PDPC source lead. Agent implication: require consent and opt-out review before direct marketing trust claims.
- Country: Multi-market Arab/MENA. Segment: all. Channel: paid ads to landing page. Confidence: A for platform policy mechanics. Evidence: Meta and TikTok policies review ads and destinations. Agent implication: trust claims on landing pages need the same review as ads.

## Avoid

- Fabricated scarcity, fake reviews, fabricated customer counts, fabricated awards, or unverifiable "trusted by" claims.
- Using government, bank, payment, or certification logos without permission and source confirmation.
- Publishing customer names, screenshots, chat logs, or reviewer identities without consent.
