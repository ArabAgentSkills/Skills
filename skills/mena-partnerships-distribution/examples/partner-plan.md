## Context

- Market: UAE.
- Partner type: ecommerce agency.
- Shared customer: ecommerce merchants needing logistics workflow automation.
- Motion: agency bundle plus referral pilot.
- Risk tier: commercial partnership with customer-data and co-marketing review needs.

## Source-backed constraints

- Public chamber and partner pages can support channel discovery, but they do not prove permission to contact members or acceptance of terms.
- Partner-program pages are examples only; they do not prove pricing, exclusivity, acceptance criteria, or legal obligations.
- UAE federal procurement and regulated workflows need separate review if the partner introduces public-sector or regulated buyers.

## Proposed pilot

1. Start with a 60-day no-exclusivity pilot.
2. Agency identifies up to five qualified ecommerce customers.
3. Vendor owns product demo, pricing, security answers, and contracting.
4. Agency owns implementation scoping and optional service bundle.
5. No customer names, logos, screenshots, or metrics are used publicly without permission.
6. Customer data is not shared until DPA/security boundaries are reviewed.

## Partner pitch draft

```text
Subject: Pilot partnership idea for UAE ecommerce operations

Hi [Name],

I am [Sender] from [Company]. We are exploring a pilot partnership for UAE ecommerce teams that need cleaner logistics workflow automation without adding manual operations work.

The working idea is:

- Shared customer: ecommerce merchants with growing delivery operations
- Customer problem: manual shipment follow-up, exception handling, and reporting gaps
- Joint offer: product workflow plus agency implementation support
- Agency value: services revenue and a clearer operations offer for existing clients
- Vendor role: demo, product onboarding, support, and commercial ownership
- Agency role: implementation scoping, configuration support, and client success input
- Pilot scope: up to five qualified accounts over 60 days, no exclusivity

Before discussing commercial terms, I would want to confirm data boundaries, brand-use permissions, and any legal/compliance review needed on both sides.

Would a short discovery call be useful?

Regards,
[Sender]
```

## Review flags

- Needs legal review before commission, resale, revenue share, or exclusivity.
- Needs privacy/security review before customer-data sharing.
- Needs brand approval before logo, customer name, or case study use.
- Needs native/operator review before Arabic or locally sensitive wording.
