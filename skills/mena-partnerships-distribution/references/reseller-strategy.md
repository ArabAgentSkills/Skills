# Reseller Strategy

Use for referral, reseller, distributor, channel, and local market-development motions.

## Default Position

- Start pilot-first.
- Use no-exclusivity as the default until performance, territory, support, legal, and commercial terms are reviewed.
- Keep partner claims narrow and evidence-based.
- Separate "can introduce customers" from "can legally and operationally sell, support, bill, and protect data."

## Fit Checklist

- Shared customer segment and sector.
- Existing customer proof or channel credibility.
- Country coverage and support capability.
- Sales qualification process.
- Implementation and customer-success ownership.
- Billing, collections, tax, invoicing, and refund responsibilities.
- Customer data access and confidentiality.
- Anti-bribery, conflict-of-interest, and public-sector exposure.

## Terms That Need Review

- Exclusivity, territory, quotas, termination, renewal, non-circumvention, and non-solicit.
- Commission, resale margin, revenue share, referral fees, or success fees.
- Sublicensing, sub-resellers, public logo use, co-branded materials, and case studies.
- Customer ownership, CRM access, support impersonation, production API access, and shared credentials.

## Output Pattern

Provide a partner scorecard:

- Fit.
- Risk.
- Proposed pilot.
- Review gates.
- Open questions.
- Next approved action.

Do not claim acceptance, legal validity, or guaranteed access.
