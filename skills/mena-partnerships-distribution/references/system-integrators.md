# Systems Integrators

Use for implementation partners, enterprise consultancies, CRM/ERP integrators, cloud partners, and local systems integrators.

## Why This Motion Is Different

Systems integrators can influence enterprise adoption, implementation quality, local support, and procurement comfort. They can also introduce risk if roles, data boundaries, security controls, and customer ownership are unclear.

## Qualification Checklist

- Target enterprise segment and countries.
- Platform or stack expertise.
- Relevant implementation proof and approved references.
- Security, privacy, and data-handling maturity.
- Support model and escalation process.
- Procurement route and approved-vendor status where relevant.
- Training and certification needs.
- Conflict with existing partners or customer obligations.

## Operating Model Questions

- Who owns discovery, statement of work, implementation, training, support, and renewal?
- Who invoices the customer?
- Who signs the DPA, security addendum, and support SLA?
- Who can access production systems, logs, or customer data?
- How are defects, incidents, and customer complaints handled?

## Review Gates

Require legal/security review for:

- Production credentials or API access.
- Customer data, logs, security artifacts, or architecture details.
- Subcontractors or offshore delivery.
- Regulated customers, government, semi-government, banks, telcos, health, education, identity, tax, payroll, or payment workflows.

## Recommended Output

Provide a partner enablement plan with training, certification, sandbox demo, implementation checklist, escalation path, and pilot criteria. Keep customer access blocked until approvals are complete.
