# Co-Marketing

Use for webinars, guides, events, chamber/community distribution, case studies, announcements, newsletters, social posts, and partner launch assets.

## Co-Marketing Intake

- Country or market.
- Audience segment and sector.
- Partner role and brand permissions.
- Channel: event, webinar, email, WhatsApp, social, landing page, press, chamber, or community.
- Assets: logos, testimonials, customer names, screenshots, metrics, quotes, or offers.
- Review owners: brand, legal, privacy, native/operator, platform, and executive.

## Rules

- No public logo use without permission.
- No customer names, screenshots, testimonials, or metrics without permission and privacy review.
- No claims of official government, bank, telco, regulator, or chamber endorsement unless documented.
- No launch, publication, audience upload, outbound send, or paid campaign without explicit approval.
- Arabic, dialect, cultural, religious, national-day, Ramadan, Eid, humor, or seniority-sensitive copy needs native/operator review.

## Safe Co-Marketing Plan

1. Define shared audience and one useful topic.
2. Create a draft asset outline.
3. Confirm brand permissions and review owners.
4. Confirm data collection, registration, consent, and follow-up rules.
5. Prepare a launch checklist with stop/rollback options.
6. Review results as aggregate data only unless consent allows more.

## Follow-Up Boundaries

Event registrants, webinar attendees, and chamber/community participants are not automatically opted into every channel. Confirm consent and channel-specific rules before email, WhatsApp, SMS, retargeting, or sales handoff.
