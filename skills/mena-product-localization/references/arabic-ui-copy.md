# Arabic UI Copy

## Defaults

- Use clear Modern Standard Arabic for cross-market products unless dialect is an intentional product choice.
- Keep action copy short and specific: pay, confirm, cancel, track, resend, verify, upload, delete, refund.
- Avoid literal English calques and fragmented translation strings.

## Pluralization

- Use CLDR/ICU or i18next plural forms for Arabic categories: zero, one, two, few, many, and other.
- Keep counts and nouns inside one translation message.
- Test counts 0, 1, 2, 3, 10, 11, 99, 100, 101, and decimals.

## Review Gates

- Native review: public UI, dialect, slogans, support macros, error messages, and user-facing checkout copy.
- Expert review: legal, tax, identity, payment, refund, cancellation, subscription, consent, and privacy copy.

## Avoid

- Gendered or honorific phrasing unless required and reviewed.
- Humor, religious language, national symbols, or dialect jokes without local review.
- Claims that Arabic legal text is compliant without counsel or official source review.
